YELLOW TEA

Yellow tea is the rarest of the six types of tea as it is complicated and time-consuming to make.

Although the manufacturing process used to develop yellow tea is incredibly similar
to the steps for green tea, there's an additional step that requires a high degree of craftsmanship.

After the withering and panning step of green tea, yellow tea undergoes wrapping
(also called heaping, yellowing, or menhuang), which causes non-enzymatic oxidation
(previously thought of as microbial fermentation).

This wrapping step brings out a more mellow flavor in tea and less astringent and vegetal notes
associated with green tea. If you are a smooth and creamy tea fan , yellow tea is for you.

The primary aim of making yellow tea is to remove the grassy smell of green tea while preserving the health properties. Yellow tea is available in different varieties, the most popular ones being:

  • Junshan yinzhen
  • Huoshan huangya
  • Beigang maojian
  • Da ye qing
  • Haimagong cha
