WHITE TEA

White tea is the least processed of the six types of tea .
Most white teas are plucked and withered(indoors and outdoors) for days,
although some white teas go through an additional drying phase at low temperatures.


White tea's minimal processing is why many companies market white tea as having high levels of
antioxidants. White tea retains the most antioxidants present in the fresh tea leaves.


Although the process seems so simple , simple does not mean easy.
Tea masters handle the leaves wuth additional care to ensure they do not bruise the leaves throughout the process.


Based on the type of tea leaves used for the tea , the white teas are named as follows :

  • Silver Needle : purely buds
  • White Peony : one bud and one or two leaves
  • Tribute Eyebrow (Gong Mei) : buds and leaves
  • Longetivity Eyebrow (Shou Mei) : mature leaves from later in the
    season
