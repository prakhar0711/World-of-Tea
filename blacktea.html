BLACK TEA

Black tea is the most consumed tea across the world;
in the US, 86% of the tea enjoyed throughout 2017 were different types of black tea, such as earl grey.

Unlike partially oxidized oolong tea, black tea is fully oxidized after a long period of withering.

Tea leaves are rolled or cut to expedite the oxidation process. Because black tea is fully oxidized,
it does not go through a separate heating stage to stop the oxidation.

Instead, it goes straight to the drying step.
Also, the oxidation process reduces tea's bitterness and increases sweetness.

A few examples of black tea are :

  • Assam Black : malty black tea from Assam , India
  • Darjeeling Black : more delicate black tea from Darjeeling , India
  • Keemum Black : sweet and fruity black tea from Anhui , China
  • Lapsang Souchong : smoky black tea fron Fujian , China
